## About Me

I am a Computing Science graduate specialising in Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Healthcare Technology.

My work focuses on applying machine learning and statistical modelling to real-world healthcare challenges, particularly neuroimaging and dementia research.

My dissertation investigates why some individuals with Mild Cognitive Impairment progress to Alzheimer's disease while others remain stable, examining the interaction between neuroimaging-derived biological ageing pace (DunedinPACNI) and cognitive reserve using the ADNI clinical dataset.

This work combines statistical modelling, moderation analysis, and machine learning, and was directly informed by neurology work-shadowing at Addenbrooke's Hospital, Cambridge.

Alongside healthcare AI research, I enjoy building full-stack applications and designing software systems that combine technical rigour with practical impact.
